# Review of Evaluation Workflow and Static Evaluation

I have reviewed your `eval.txt` workflow. Decoupling the `pass` move completely out of `moves.py` and making it an inherent structural feature of the Alpha-Beta DFS is a solid architectural decision. 

Here is my detailed review of both your workflow and your evaluation heuristic ideas.

## 1. The Pass Move in Alpha-Beta Workflow

**Pros:**
- It keeps your `moves.py` bit-packing clean (100% board intersections).
- It natively and cleanly handles the termination condition recursively. 

**Cons / Architecture Tweaks:**
- **Search Ordering (Important):** Alpha-Beta pruning is exceptionally dependent on move ordering. If you evaluate `pass` *first* at every single node, you will establish an artificially low/terrible bound during the mid-game (since passing mid-game is an awful play), significantly reducing your pruning efficiency. I heavily recommend evaluating `pass` *after* generating and evaluating the highest priority legal moves, or only if no good moves exist.
- **Sentinel Value:** Instead of using `previous_move = None`, use an explicit constant like `PASS_MOVE = 0xFFFF` (or `361`). This prevents `NoneType` attribute errors down the line and makes the termination condition an extremely fast and safe integer comparison: `if pos.previous_move == PASS_MOVE and pos.parent.previous_move == PASS_MOVE: return score`.

## 2. Feature Integration: Static Eval vs. Move Sorting

It is crucial to distinguish between properties of a **Static State** and properties of a **Transition (Move)**:

### What belongs in `static_eval(position)` 
*(Evaluating the board as it sits, irrespective of the move played)*
1. **Territory Estimation:** Perfect territory counting is far too slow for deep leaf nodes. Instead, use a lightweight **Influence Map**. Traverse empty spaces and quickly assess surrounding stones to determine rudimentary control.
2. **Weak vs Strong Groups (Proximity):** You mentioned "proximity to weak groups." Statically, just map the liberties of all groups. Penalize the evaluation heavily if your groups have `1` or `2` liberties (weak). Reward the score for opponent groups with few liberties.
3. **Total Captures:** **Crucial missing piece:** Your current `Position` and `BitBoard` objects *do not track total captured stones* over the duration of the game! You need to add a running tally to `Position` (e.g., `black_captures` tally). Inside `perform_captures` in `moves.py`, increment this tally based on the exact number of stones erased.

### What belongs in `moves.py -> assign_priority`
*(Evaluating the quality of the move itself to search it sooner)*
1. **Shape Moves (jumps, diagonals):** The static evaluator shouldn't look for move "shapes" natively—that's inefficient. Instead, in `give_attributes()` inside `moves.py`, check the relative geometry of the move to nearby friendly stones. If it creates a beautiful shape, allocate a priority bit.
2. **Size of Capture:** You suggested prioritizing a "capture of 5 stones". Since you have 9 free bits remaining in your `uint16` move wrapper, you can assign 3 bits to store the *number of stones captured*, forcing Alpha-Beta to always evaluate massive captures before 1-stone captures!
3. **Endgame Value (Sente/Gote):** Sente generally means keeping the initiative. Moves that put the opponent in Atari are purely Sente. You are already handling this flawlessly by prioritizing `is_atari_on_opponent` via bit-shifting!
